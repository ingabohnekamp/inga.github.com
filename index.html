ABOUT / INGA BOHNEKAMP / YOGA, MINDFULNESS, PSYCHOLOGY

Inga Bohnekamp is an internationally trained psychologist (Dipl.Psych., Germany) with post-graduate training in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectic Behavioral Therapy (DBT) and Mindfulness. She is a graduate from the Strala Yoga 200hr Yoga Teacher Training with Tara Stiles in New York City as well as a trained Yoga Teacher for Children and Adolescents (Kinderyogaakademie).

Inga is the author of two published Children’s Books, Jasper’s Journey to the Yoga Animals/ Jaspers Reise zu den Yoga Tieren (bilingual) and Felix und die Feuerschlange (Felix and the Snake of Fire, German), with her third book The Colours of My Rainbow coming up this year in cooperation with the Children’s Hospital of Eastern Ontario (CHEO, Ottawa, Canada). She regularly contributes to Attachment Parenting International’s Blog APT’LYSaid on Mindful Parenting topics and speaks and guides workshops for professionals at international conferences and events.

Merging her academic and therapeutic background in psychology and different psychotherapy approaches with yoga and mindfulness allows Inga to create a holistic and deeply intuitive MindBodyEmotion approach which she brings into and expresses in all aspects of her work. Inga is the founder and Program Director of the MAPLE MINDS Mindfulness, Yoga and Relaxation for Kids and Teens with Chronic Illness Program for the CHEO.

She offers trainings, workshops as well as targeted individual and group work for children, teenagers and adults in various settings including hospitals, ambulatory care centres and schools as well as private sessions in her clients’ own homes.

Inga is fluent in English, German and French and experienced working in multicultural and multinational settings.
